Early Voting in SC Rose Dramatically From 2016

Written By: Tremain Prioleau II

November 12th, 2020

Image Source: MGN Online

Early voting turnout in South Carolina this election season outpaced the 2016 election with more than a million voters voting absentee. This change in voter behavior is a result of the Covid-19 pandemic. South Carolina was previously a state that prohibited “no excuse” absentee voting. This year the state allowed everyone to vote absentee through the measures of a “State of Emergency” as their excuse.

Many voters opted to vote by mail in order to stay out of election day crowds while others still voted in person at their local county board of elections office.

Chris Whitmire, Director of Public Information for the State Election Commission, noted that the state has not seen this many people voting before election day saying “It was almost like every day was Election Day.” While this was a success, Governor McMaster is not in support of early voting in the future.
